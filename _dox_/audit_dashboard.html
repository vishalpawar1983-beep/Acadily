Monthly: Daybook vs Fee Records (VM Only) i
×This compares what the receptionist logged manually (Daybook, cyan bars) against what got officially receipted in the system (Fee Payments, green bars) each month.

When bars match = clean month. When cyan is taller = cash was collected but not all of it was formally receipted. Look for months where the gap is large — those need investigation.

Fee Status: Collected / Outstanding / Discount i
×Shows where all the money stands across all 99 students:

Collected — actual money received and receipted.
Outstanding — fees owed but not yet paid by students.
Discounts — fee reductions given to students (never collectible).

A large outstanding slice means a lot of money is still owed. A large discount slice means the previous owner gave heavy concessions — verify if those discounts were legitimate or used to hide collections.

Monthly Gap (Daybook Credit − Fee Payments) i
×Each bar = Daybook credit minus Fee payments for that month.

Red bars (positive) — more cash was logged by the receptionist than got receipted. This could be cash payments not formally entered, or non-fee income slipping in.
Green bars (zero/near-zero) — clean month, everything reconciles.

Months with large red bars are where you should check: did students pay cash that was never receipted?

Unearned Revenue by Category i
×Money collected by the previous owner before March 6 for classes that happen after March 6 — meaning you bear the cost of delivering those classes.

Cat A (Fully Paid) — highest risk. The student paid everything already. You teach them for free.
Cat B (Partial) — some fees collected before cutoff, some still owed. You deliver classes but can still collect the remaining balance.
Cat C (No Records) — students with no fee records at all. If they actually paid cash, that money went to the previous owner with no trace.
